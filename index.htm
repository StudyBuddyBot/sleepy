How does it work?
It's very easy!
When sleeping, a person goes through several cycles of alternating slow and fast phases of sleep. If you wake up during the slow phase, you will feel sleep-deprived and sleep-deprived, which can be avoided by waking up at the end of the fast phase of sleep.

Each phase of sleep lasts an average of 90 minutes, and in order for an adult to get a good night's sleep, 5-6 such phases are necessary, which corresponds to 7-9 hours of sleep.
